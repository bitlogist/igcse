Plant Transport

Vascular Bundles

Plants have vascular bundles which transport nutrients throughout the plant.

  • Xylem transport water
  • Phloem transport sucrose and amino acids

Xylem

Xylem cells are dead and hollow. They transport water up the plant in one direction.

  • Are usually near the outside of the stem
  • Are lined with lignin which makes them strong
  • More central than phloem
  • Have no nucleus present

Phloem

Phloem cells transport sucrose and amino acids from sources to sinks.

  • Carry sucrose and amino acids using translocation
  • Are usually near the outside of the stem

Transpiration

Transpiration is the loss of water through the leaves as water vapour due to evaporation.

  • Root hair cells absorb water (water uptake) via osmosis
  • Water travels up the xylem due to a difference in water potential
  • Water diffuses into the mesophyll layer
  • Water evaporates as water vapour through the stomata
