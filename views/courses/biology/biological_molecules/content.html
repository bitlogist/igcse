Biological Molecules

Types of Biological Molecules

There are 3 main types of biological molecules in living organisms.
  • Carbohydrates
  • Proteins
  • Lipids

Carbohydrates

Carbohydrates are made of only 3 obvious elements.

  • Carbon
  • Hydrogen
  • Oxygen

There are different types of carbohydrates that are classified by their complexity.

  • Monosaccharides
    • Glucose
    • Fructose
  • Disaccharides
    • Maltose
    • Sucrose
  • Polysaccharides
    • Starch
    • Glycogen

Monosaccharides are simple sugars made up of a single sugar molecule. All monosaccharides are reducing sugars.

Enzymes

Enzymes are biological catalysts and proteins. Enzymes increase the rate of reaction without being used up. Enzymes have the job of converting substrate molecules into product molecules.

  • Substrate: the reactant molecules that bind to the enzyme
  • Product: the finished product of the reaction

The Active Site

All enzymes have an active site that is specific to a particular substrate.

  • The shape of the active site is complementary to the shape of the substrate
  • A different substrate can't bind to the active site of an enzyme if their shapes do not match
  • Changing the shape of the active site will prevent it from converting substrate into product

Denatured Enzymes

The denaturation of an enzyme is irreversible. It significantly reduces the rate of reaction.

  1. Enzyme begins to denature
  2. The shape of the active site changes and is no longer complementary to the substrate
  3. The enzyme can no longer convert substrate into product
  4. This causes the rate of reaction to decrease
