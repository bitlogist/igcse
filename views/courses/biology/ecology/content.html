Ecology

Ecosystems

An ecosystem is a collection of living organisms and their environment interacting in a given area.

  • Animals are living organisms
  • Plants are organisms that provide food for animals
  • Rocks and other lifeless entities make up the environment

Populations

A population is a group of organisms from the same species, living in the same area at the same time.

Community

A community is all of the populations of different species in an ecosystem.

Energy Transfer

All organisms need energy, and the Sun is the principle source of energy. All organisms need sunlight for nutrients or warmth.

Food Chains

A food chain is a diagram that shows the linear transfer of energy from one organism to another. All food chains start with a plant called the producer because it provides food for other organisms using sunlight and nutrients from the soil.

 Role Description
Producer This is an organism that produces its own food.
Primary Consumer This is a herbivore because it only eats plants.
Secondary Consumer This is a predator that feeds on primary consumers.
Tertiary Consumer This is a predator that feeds on secondary consumers.
Decomposer This is an organism that recycles dead organisms into raw nutrients.

The Sun is not included in a food chain. Here is an example of a food chain starting with the producer.

  1. Grass (Producer)
  2. Rabbit (Primary Consumer)
  3. Snake (Secondary Consumer)

Food Webs

A food web is a group of interconnected food chains. It is a more accurate representation of the energy flow in a community.

Trophic Levels

A trophic level is the group of organisms that fall under a category such as producers or primary consumers in a food chain. Energy is lost between each trophic level usually resulting in less organisms higher up in the food chain. In fact, it is rare for a food chain to be longer than 5 organisms.

Energy can be lost in many different ways.

  • Movement
  • Excretion
  • Egestion
  • Heat Loss
