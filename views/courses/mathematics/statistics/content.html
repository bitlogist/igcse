Averages

The average usually refers to the mean. However, in statistics, there are 3 types of averages.

  • Mean: sum over count
  • Median: middle value
  • Mode: most common value

The 3 averages are designed to work on lists, but it is possible to use them on frequency tables as well. There are 3 types of quantitative datasets.

  • List (Linear)
  • Ungrouped Frequency Table (Discrete Table)
  • Grouped Frequency Table (Continuous Table)

The Mean

The mean is calculated by dividing the total of the items by the number of items. The principle is the same across all datasets because a grouped frequency table can be converted to an ungrouped one which can then be simplified to a list of numbers.

The mean of a list is simple. Divide the sum of the list or set of numbers by the length of the list or set.

\( \overline{A}=\frac{\sum{A}}{\textrm{n}(A)} \)

The mean of ungrouped data can be accurately found by converting it to a list through repetition. However, it is quicker to divide the sum of each pair's product by the sum of the frequencies.

\( \overline{A}=\frac{\sum{A_{x} \times A_{f}}}{\sum{A_{f}}} \)

Frequency is a count of a specific star rating in a list.

\( \overline{A}=\frac{0 \times 2 + 1 \times 1 \ldots 5 \times 4}{2 + 1 + 0 + 1 + 2 + 4} \)

The list form is 0, 0, 1, 3, 4, 4, 5, 5, 5, 5, 5. A frequency table simply condenses a list of numeric values.

The mean of grouped data is an estimated mean because the data has lost accuracy. The use of ranges is a form of lossy compression in statistics. A range does not contain information about the distribution of values in the range. The mean of the lower and upper bounds is treated as a value instead of the range. This ultimately transforms the ungrouped dataset into a grouped dataset.

\( A_{m}=\frac{A_{l}+A_{u}}{2} \)

The mean or midpoint of the lower bound and upper bound is used to convert the grouped frequency table into an approximate ungrouped table.

\( \overline{A}=\frac{\sum{A_{m} \times A_{f}}}{\sum{A_{f}}} \)
