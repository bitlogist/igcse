Magnetic Properties

There are different types of metals with different magnetic properties. Only a few metals are magnetic and some are better suited for making a typical magnet.

Magnetic Materials

Magnetic materials can be attracted by magnets but not repelled. Magnetic hardness is a measure of how easily a magnetic material can be magnetised. Iron is magnetically soft while steel is hard. This means it is harder to change the magnetic field of steel objects.

  • Iron (Ferrous)
  • Cobalt
  • Nickel

Steel is an alloy of iron. Alloys can inherit some properties like magnetism. Alloys containing iron are called ferrous.

Permanent Magnets

Permanent magnets last a long time like typical bar magnets, horseshoe magnets, and refrigerator magnets. They are harder to magnetise and demagnetise. It is important to remember that the magnetic field flows from north to south!

  • Hard Magnetic Material (Steel)
  • North Pole & South Pole
  • Attract & Repel

Electromagnets

Electromagnets are usually temporary magnets made by passing electricity through a coil.

Grip Rule

The right-hand grip rule is used to determine the rotation of a magnetic field produced by a current flowing through a wire. The thumb points in the direction of the current while the fingers curl in the direction of the magnetic field.

Fleming's Hand Rules

Fleming's hand rules are used to determine the relative directions of a current, force, and magnetic field. Fleming's left-hand rule applies to situations where a force is being produced by a current. Fleming's right-hand rule applies to situations where a current is being produced by a force. In both cases, the thumb is the force, the index finger is the magnetic field, and the middle finger is the direction of the current.
